I recently finished *"Achieving Fame and Fortune in America"* (美利坚名利双收) by 白色十三号 (White Thirteen), and I have to say, it really hit the spot. It's been a while since I read a US entertainment industry novel this engaging.

The premise is quite unique: A Chinese guy who was just a "Beipiao" (someone from outside Beijing struggling to make it) transmigrates into the body of a dirt-poor white guy in 2003 America. Talk about a "hell start"! Right from the beginning, the author does a fantastic job of portraying the squalor of the slums and the protagonist's desperate situation – it really pulls you in and makes you feel his predicament.

What follows is a fascinating, no-holds-barred fight for survival and a climb up the social ladder. The protagonist is incredibly driven and resourceful, but he's not afraid to get his hands dirty and use unconventional, even questionable, methods. Watching him secure his first pot of gold through "grey area" tactics, like using a "civil rights" angle to boost business for a strip club to pay off his debt, is wild and entertaining. His journey into Hollywood – using backdoor connections, networking with producers, starting with B-movies, and working his way up from extra to supporting actor – perfectly captures the gritty, desperate struggle of someone clawing their way up from the absolute bottom. While exaggerated at times, these parts are incredibly fun to read.

白色十三号 is clearly skilled at writing about bottom-tier characters. The protagonist isn't a traditional hero – he's purely utilitarian and often ruthless in his pursuit of fame and fortune – but the author makes his motivations understandable given his dire circumstances. The depiction of 2003 America and especially the Hollywood scene feels surprisingly rich and detailed. The author doesn't shy away from showing the infighting, the power plays, and the transactions behind the glamour, offering a raw, unfiltered look at the industry.

The writing style is concise and has a good dose of humor, keeping the tone from becoming too heavy despite the dark subject matter. The pacing is tight and keeps the story moving forward quickly, and the characters feel well-developed. The author really manages to convey the desperate ambition of someone from the bottom.

## Important Caveats for Readers
Now, this book definitely isn't for everyone. The protagonist's purely self-interested and often amoral actions are central to the plot. If you prefer protagonists with a strong moral compass, you might find him off-putting. Additionally, the book contains a fair amount of explicit content ("low-brow stuff") and has a significant focus on relationships/sexual encounters, often described with the slang term "bulldozer" (implying multiple partners). The depiction of power and sex exchanges is quite direct. So, if you are uncomfortable with morally grey protagonists or explicit content, you should approach with caution.

However, if you enjoy fast-paced, entertaining stories about climbing the ladder in a difficult world, appreciate a cunning and ruthless protagonist, and aren't bothered by explicit content and moral ambiguity, then this book is absolutely worth checking out. Watching a character from the absolute bottom make his way to fame and fortune in Hollywood, using every trick in the book, is incredibly satisfying. It's a strong return to the US entertainment genre for 白色十三号.
